# Product brief: Halftone Press

A clearly-labelled FICTIONAL studio, built as the proof site for the agentic-dev1
website infrastructure. The risograph process it describes is real and described
truthfully; the studio, its editions, and its copy are invented for the demo. No
testimonials, credentials, or metrics are fabricated.

## What

A one-page marketing site for a small risograph print studio: what riso is, the
three-pass process, the kinds of editions it prints, and an order form. A visitor
reads how the process works and starts an edition.

## Who

A designer or small publisher who wants short-run, spot-colour printing with the
hand-fed riso look (overprint, grain, deliberate misregistration), and needs to
understand the process and how to commission a run.

## Success metric

Proof-site purpose: it clears the website definition of done
(`docs/website-build-workflow.md`). Concretely: the gate suite is green (axe 0
serious/critical, Lighthouse perf >= 90 / a11y >= 95, static gates pass), the
design is distinct (not one of the three AI looks, deliberate Space Grotesk / IBM
Plex pairing, a real overprint signature), and it renders in light and dark at
~390px and ~1280px. There is no analytics layer; this is a static demo.

## Scope cut

Single page, no CMS, no real order backend (the form posts nowhere), no e-commerce.
The point is to prove the infrastructure produces a distinctive, market-floor site
from a fresh design pass, not to run a print shop.
